Bahrain is one of the most historically rich countries in the Gulf — and most of it sits quietly, waiting to be discovered. Tap each category to explore.

Forts
Ancient fortresses & UNESCO heritage
⌄

Bahrain Fort (Qal'at al-Bahrain)

A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the oldest forts in the Gulf. Layers of civilisation dating back 4,000 years.

Go at golden hour — the light over the sea is unreal.

Arad Fort

A beautifully restored Portuguese-era fort in Muharraq. Small but atmospheric, especially at dusk with the water behind it.

Combine with a walk along the Muharraq corniche.

Riffa Fort

Perched on a cliff overlooking Hunanaiya Valley, this fort was once home to Bahraini rulers. Stunning views and rich history.

Less crowded than Bahrain Fort — a hidden gem.

Qalaat Bu Maher

A coastal fort in Muharraq partially submerged at high tide, giving it an almost mythical appearance. Accessible only by boat from Bahrain National Museum.

Ask at the museum about boat trips — it's worth every minute.

Bahrain National Museum

The museum houses artefacts from Dilmun civilisation and brings Bahrain's ancient story to life beautifully — and is the starting point for the Bu Maher boat ride.

Spend at least 2 hours here — there's more than you'd expect.

Nature
Desert wonders & natural landmarks
⌄

Tree of Life

A 400-year-old mesquite tree standing alone in the middle of the desert — no water source, no explanation. One of Bahrain's most mystical spots.

Best visited at sunset or under a full moon.

Al Bilaj Beach

One of Bahrain's most serene and unspoilt beaches — far from the crowds, with calm clear water and raw natural beauty.

Perfect for a quiet sunrise or sunset — bring your own refreshments.

Al Areen Wildlife Park

Bahrain's only wildlife sanctuary — home to Arabian oryx, gazelles, ostriches and more roaming freely across a protected desert reserve.

Go in the morning when animals are most active.

Crafts
Pottery, weaving & traditional arts
⌄

Pottery Village — A'ali

The oldest continuously working pottery village in the Gulf. Watch local craftsmen shape clay the same way their ancestors did centuries ago.

You can try making your own piece at some workshops.

Alnaseej Textile Factory

A traditional textile factory in Muharraq where craftsmen weave fabric on ancient wooden looms. A living piece of Bahraini heritage.

Go on a weekday morning to see the weavers actively working.

Adventure & Sports
Thrills, outdoor experiences & activities
⌄

Gravity Indoor Skydiving

Bahrain's wind tunnel experience — the closest thing to freefall without jumping out of a plane. Great for all ages.

Book in advance — slots fill up fast on weekends.

Horse Riding

Ride through Bahrain's desert landscape — guided rides at sunrise or sunset across open terrain.

Early morning rides are cooler and the desert light is beautiful.

Cycling

Bahrain has a growing cycling scene with dedicated tracks and open coastal roads.

Sheikh Nasser Loop in Zallaq is the most scenic route — flat, coastal and stunning at golden hour.

Kayaking & SUP

Paddle through Bahrain's calm coastal waters and mangroves, or try stand up paddleboarding on the flat calm sea.

The mangroves in Tubli Bay are stunning. Sunset SUP sessions are a must.

Boat Rides

Explore Bahrain's coastline and nearby islands by boat — a completely different perspective of the island.

Sunset boat tours are especially magical.

Go Karting & F1 Circuit

World class karting facilities near the iconic F1 circuit. Great for a competitive day out with friends or family.

Bahrain International Circuit offers karting on race weekends.

Traditional Restaurants
Breakfast, grills & seafood — the Bahraini way
⌄
Breakfast

Hajis

حاجي

A legendary Bahraini breakfast institution in old Manama. Locals have been coming here for generations.

@hajiscafe

Danet AlTawawish Restaurant & Cafe

دانة الطواويش

A traditional Bahraini restaurant in the heart of Manama. The kind of place where recipes have been passed down through families and every meal feels homemade — must try their Mahyawa bread with cheese (مهياوة وجبن).

📍 View on Google Maps

Baba Taher

بابا طاهر

A beloved old-school Bahraini bakery and sweet shop. Famous for its fresh pastries and traditional Bahraini sweets, it's a neighbourhood staple that has stood the test of time.

@baba_taher_restaurant

Emmawash

إموش

A traditional Bahraini seafood restaurant rooted in the island's fishing heritage. Known for its rich, spiced fish dishes served over rice — a true taste of coastal Bahrain.

@emmawash_bh

Darseen Cafe

دارسين

A charming cafe nestled inside the Bahrain National Museum, known for its warm cinnamon-infused drinks and traditional Bahraini breakfast spread. A peaceful spot after exploring the museum.

@darseencafebh

Sharbet Saffron Cafe

شربت زعفران

A well-loved Bahraini breakfast cafe with a loyal local following. Famous for its golden saffron drinks and traditional morning dishes that have made it a daily ritual for many Bahrainis.

@sharbet_saffron
Grills

Tren Tren

One of Bahrain's most celebrated grill spots, best known for their Riyash — juicy, perfectly charred lamb chops that keep people coming back.

The Riyash is the star — don't leave without ordering it.

@trentren.bh

Bu Jamal

A beloved grill institution in Riffa. Their tikka is famously juicy, and the spicy yogurt tikka is a standout dish you won't find done better elsewhere.

Ask for the spicy yogurt tikka — it's the one to order.

📍 View on Google Maps

BBQ Town

A one-of-a-kind outdoor dining experience in Bilad Al Qadeem — part garden restaurant, part food village. Their Rughab (رقبة) — neck meat slow-cooked in BBQ sauce — is buttery, tender and unforgettable.

Go with a group so you can try as many dishes as possible.

@bbq_.town

Saber Ayoob

A reliable Bahraini grill chain found all around the island, making it an easy go-to for quality tikka, kebab, and grills.

Great for a quick, no-fuss grill fix anywhere in Bahrain.

@sabarayoobrestaurant

Kwar

Found in Hamad Town, Souq Al Baraha, Riffa and Hoora. Their cheesy scrambled eggs are legendary and the tikka maqliya (fried tikka) is crispy and deeply flavourful.

The cheesy scrambled eggs and fried tikka are not to be missed.

@alkwar

Khan Baghdad

An Iraqi cuisine gem in Bahrain, known for its authentic Reghab (رقبة) and its Iraqi kebab, which has a distinct flavour unlike anything else on the island.

The Iraqi kebab is unlike any other kebab in Bahrain — a must try.

@khan_bghdad
Seafood

Asmak Alseef

A great seafood destination, particularly known for its seabass, seabream, and safee fish (سمج صافي).

The seabass and safee fish (سمج صافي) are the ones to order.

@alseef.fish

Fish Line

Located on Budaiya Road, Fish Line offers a comprehensive seafood menu covering all the classic Bahraini fish dishes.

A solid all-rounder — good for the whole family.

@fishline.bh

Tabreez

A sprawling outdoor garden restaurant that doubles as a seafood market. Famous for safee fish (سمج صافي) served with muhammar rice (عيش محمر) — sweet, savoury and deeply Bahraini.

The safee fish with muhammar rice is the signature dish.

@tabreez_restaurant
Cafes & Markets
Local favourites & hidden gems
⌄

Farmers Market — Hoorat Aali

A vibrant weekly market in the village of Aali featuring fresh local produce, homemade goods, and artisan crafts.

Go early on market day — the best stalls sell out fast.
