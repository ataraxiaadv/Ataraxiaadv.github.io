The Adventures

Every trip that shaped the way I see the world — and the ones still to come.

I didn't plan any of this. I just kept saying yes — to the detour, to the stranger's invitation, to the country nobody in my circle had been to. Most of these trips were with my travel buddy or a group of girls who trusted each other enough to get lost together.

These aren't highlights. They're the real ones — the wrong turns, the glacier road closures, the yurts, the local grandmothers who fed us, the moments that still show up in my mind at 2am for no reason.

🇮🇸 Iceland — Top 1

Four girls, one car, nine nights, and a glacier that closed the road

Group of 4 Rent a car Ring Road Hiking

We planned to do the full Ring Road. On day seven, a glacier closed it. We turned back and didn't care — we'd already hiked every waterfall on the south coast, camped under the northern lights, stood inside a crater, and driven roads that felt like the end of the earth. Iceland doesn't need a complete plan. It just needs you to show up.

  • Hiked Skógafoss, Seljalandsfoss and waterfalls with no names
  • Golden Circle in one day — Þingvellir, Geysir, Gullfoss
  • Woke up to the northern lights outside the car
  • Road closed by glacier on day 7 — turned back without regret
  • 9 nights — still not enough

🇰🇬 Kyrgyzstan — Top 2

The whole country in one trip — the landscape is violent and the people are everything

Full country Yurts Hiking Off grid

We went around the whole country. The landscape is the kind that makes you feel genuinely small — in the best way. We lived in yurts, hiked places that don't appear on most maps, and rode horses across open steppe. The city wasn't for us and the food was meh — but the mountains, the nomadic culture and the silence of being somewhere truly remote made it one of the most remarkable trips I've done.

  • Crossed the full country — north to south
  • Stayed in yurts with nomadic families
  • Hiked Song-Köl lake — no other tourists in sight
  • Remarkable landscape at every turn
  • One of the most affordable trips — quality stays, zero waste

🇱🇰 Sri Lanka — Top 3

Lived in the jungle, surfed at dawn, hiked the end of the world

Local life Jungle stay Surfing Hiking

We lived with a Sri Lankan family in the jungle. Not a resort, not a hotel — a family home surrounded by trees and sounds I'd never heard before. We surfed in Weligama at dawn, beached in Mirissa, rode the famous train through Nuwara Eliya in the cold misty highlands, cooked their food, did a village tour, and hiked to what locals call the End of the World. The food was extraordinary. The kindness of the people even more so.

  • Stayed with a local Sri Lankan family in the jungle
  • Surfed in Weligama — complete beginners welcome
  • Train through Nuwara Eliya — cold, misty, unforgettable
  • Hiked the End of the World — worth every step
  • Ella, Colombo, Mirissa — covered it all

🇵🇹 Portugal — Madeira

Lisbon, then Madeira by plane, then Porto by train — one country, three worlds

Road trip Hiking Black sand beach Train

Portugal surprised us every time we changed cities. Lisbon first — beautiful and overwhelming. Then a short flight to Madeira, where we hiked almost every trail on the island and found a black sand beach that felt like another planet. Then the train to Porto, slow and scenic. Three completely different experiences in one country — none of them tourist traps.

  • Madeira — hiked almost every levada trail
  • Black sand beach in Madeira — completely unexpected
  • Lisbon → Madeira → Porto — three cities, one trip
  • Porto by train from Lisbon — scenic and easy

🇲🇻 Maldives

Five girls, one island, zero other guests — we felt like we owned the place

Group of 5 Diving Sharks Affordable resort

Everyone assumes the Maldives is only for honeymoons with unlimited budgets. We found a beautiful, affordable resort where we were essentially the only guests — five girls who felt like they owned an entire island. We dived with sharks, snorkelled every morning, watched sunsets from the water and never once felt like we were missing anything the expensive places have. It was one of the most peaceful trips I've ever done.

  • Found an amazing affordable resort — practically had it to ourselves
  • Dived with reef sharks — completely calm, completely beautiful
  • Snorkelling every single morning before breakfast
  • Five girls, total peace, zero regrets

🇵🇭 Philippines

Based in Batangas, went everywhere — Tatay Falls, Coron, and a grandmother's table

Island hopping Snorkelling Local family Road trips

We lived in Batangas as our base — not a resort, just a home. From there we went everywhere: Tatay Falls, island hopping to nearby islands, snorkelling in water so clear it didn't feel real. We visited a friend's grandmother in the province, ate at her table, did road trips on roads with no names. The clearest coral I've ever seen. The warmest people I've ever met.

  • Tatay Falls — reached by boat and jungle trail
  • Island hopping — the water is unreal
  • Snorkelled in the clearest coral reef I've ever seen
  • Visited a local family in Batangas — ate home-cooked food
  • Road trips with no destination — just driving and stopping

🇮🇹 Italy

Florence, Rome, Lake Como — and we accidentally drove into Switzerland

Road trip Rock climbing Lake Como Unplanned

We started in Florence, walked to the Duomo at night when the crowds were gone, drove to Rome at sunrise for the Colosseum, then headed north toward Milan. Somewhere near Lake Como we stopped to rock climb — one of those completely unplanned moments that ends up being the one you remember most. We then drove in the wrong direction and entered Switzerland by accident. We turned around immediately but still count it as a third country.

  • Florence Duomo at night — completely worth staying up for
  • Colosseum at dawn — almost nobody there
  • Rock climbing at Lake Como — spontaneous and incredible
  • Accidentally drove into Switzerland — unplanned Switzerland visit
  • Florence → Rome → Milan in one road trip

🇴🇲 Oman — Regular

Wadis, Dimaniyat Islands, turtles, and Souq Muttrah — a neighbour worth revisiting

Wadis Diving Islands Regular trip

Oman is close enough to visit regularly — and we do. The wadis are some of the most beautiful natural swimming spots in the region. Dimaniyat Islands is a marine reserve where you swim with turtles and coral that rivals anywhere in the world. Souq Muttrah in Muscat is the kind of market that makes you feel like you've stepped back 200 years. A neighbour that never gets old.

  • Wadi Shab and Wadi Bani Khalid — worth every trip
  • Dimaniyat Islands — turtles, coral, complete peace
  • Souq Muttrah — Muscat's ancient market
  • Close enough for a long weekend — far enough to feel like another world

🇹🇷 Turkey — Regular

A hut in Sapanca, horse riding in Gebze, and a horse that threw me off in the middle of nowhere

Regular trip Road trips Horse riding Hiking

Turkey is one of those places we keep going back to. Once we stayed in a hut bungalow in Sapanca with a group of friends — cold mornings, the hosts making a full Turkish breakfast, wood stove, complete silence. Another time I went horse riding in Gebze and the horse threw me off in the middle of nowhere. Alone. I walked back. We also drove to Bursa for snow, and motorskiied somewhere that shouldn't be forgotten.

  • Hut bungalow in Sapanca — woke up to homemade Turkish breakfast
  • Horse riding in Gebze — got thrown off, walked back alone
  • Drove to Bursa for snow and mountain air
  • Motorskiing — one of the most fun things we've done
  • Istanbul regulalry — always finds something new

🇪🇸 Spain

Hiked Málaga, drove to Tarifa, got lost in Andalucía, played golf twice in Marbella

Travel buddy Road trip Hiking Marbella

Me and my travel buddy drove the south of Spain together. Hiked in Málaga, then drove the coast to Tarifa — the southernmost point of Europe, where you can see Africa across the water. Stopped to meet a friend in Andalucía, then on to Ronda for the famous bridge. Ended in Marbella at my aunt's house where we explored, drove around with people who had very nice cars, and played golf twice. Neither of us knew how to play golf.

  • Hiked in Málaga — underrated trail views
  • Tarifa — saw Africa from Europe across the strait
  • Ronda — the bridge is as dramatic as it looks
  • Marbella — stayed with family, explored like locals
  • Played golf twice — no regrets, would do again

🇹🇭 Thailand

Phuket, Krabi, Bangkok — great food, great islands, too many tourists

Island hopping Food Shopping Busy

Thailand is fun — genuinely fun. Phuket has some of the best street food I've eaten anywhere. Krabi is beautiful for island hopping even if you share the water with 200 other boats. Bangkok is great for shopping and the night markets are worth every minute. Honest verdict: it's busy, it's touristy, and it's still worth going. Just manage your expectations and go in the shoulder season.

  • Phuket — best street food of the trip
  • Krabi — island hopping, a lot of boats but beautiful water
  • Bangkok night markets — go with an empty stomach
  • Honest take — very busy, still worth it
