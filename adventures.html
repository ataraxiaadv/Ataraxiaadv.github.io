🇮🇸 Iceland

Where the sun never sets and silence is deafening

Solo Hiking Camping Rent a car

I drove the Ring Road alone. No itinerary, no bookings — just a rental car, a sleeping bag, and the kind of quiet you can only find at 2am when the sun is still painting the sky pink. Iceland doesn't let you be indifferent. It either breaks you open or puts you back together. For me, it did both.

  • Slept in the car under the northern lights near Kirkjufell
  • Hiked to Skógafoss at sunrise — completely alone
  • Got lost on the Westfjords and found the most beautiful fjord I've ever seen
  • Total budget: under €800 for 10 days including flights

🇰🇬 Kyrgyzstan

Woke up in a tent with no plan and found everything

Solo Trekking Camping Off grid

Kyrgyzstan is what happens when you go somewhere nobody told you to go. The mountains are violent and beautiful. The people are among the most generous I've ever encountered. I slept in yurts, trekked for days without seeing another tourist, and drank fermented mare's milk at 3,000 metres. It changed something in me permanently.

  • 3-day trek to Song-Köl lake — no crowds, just horses and sky
  • Stayed with a nomadic family in a yurt near Karakol
  • Hitchhiked across the Torugart Pass
  • One of the cheapest countries I've ever visited

🇵🇹 Portugal

Slow mornings, cold Atlantic, and the best €2 coffee of my life

Solo Coastal drive Local life Rent a car

Portugal surprised me. I expected beaches and tourists — I found fishing villages where time moves differently, cliffs that make you feel genuinely small, and a culture that still values sitting down and doing nothing. I drove from Porto to the Algarve with no fixed stops. I'm still thinking about a particular sunset in Sagres.

  • Rented a car in Porto — best decision of the trip
  • Spent 3 nights in a tiny fishing village near Nazaré
  • Hiked the Rota Vicentina coastal trail section
  • Avoided Lisbon tourist spots entirely — found better ones

🇮🇹 Italy

Got lost on a backroad and found a village that didn't have Wi-Fi

Solo Road trip Food Off the beaten path

Everyone goes to Rome and Florence. I rented a car and drove through Calabria, Basilicata and the Dolomites — regions most tourists fly over. I ate in places with no English menu, stayed in family-run guesthouses that cost €30 a night, and drove mountain roads that should probably be illegal. The Italy nobody talks about is the best Italy.

  • Drove the Stelvio Pass — one of the greatest roads on earth
  • Stayed 2 nights in Matera — the city carved into rock
  • Found a village in Calabria where I was the only non-local
  • Ate the best food of my life for under €15 a day

🇵🇭 Philippines

7,000 islands and I only needed three to understand the world differently

Solo Island hopping Diving Local boats

The Philippines is chaos and paradise existing simultaneously. I took local ferries between islands, avoided the tourist resorts entirely, and ended up in places so beautiful they felt unreal. The water is the kind of blue that makes you question every ocean you've seen before. The people will make you want to stay forever.

  • Took a local bangka boat to an uninhabited island near Palawan
  • Spent a week in Siargao before it became famous
  • Dove with whale sharks in Donsol — no crowds, no cages
  • Total cost: one of the most affordable trips I've done

🇲🇻 Maldives

You don't need a resort to experience the most beautiful water on earth

Solo Local islands Budget Snorkelling

Everyone thinks the Maldives is only for honeymooners with unlimited budgets. I went solo, stayed on local islands, paid €20 a night for a guesthouse, and swam in the same water as the €1,000-a-night overwater bungalows. The Maldives nobody talks about — the local island version — is actually better. Quieter, more real, infinitely more interesting.

  • Stayed on Maafushi and Dhigurah local islands
  • Snorkelled with mantas and reef sharks every morning
  • Ate local food for €3 a meal at family-run cafes
  • Total trip cost: fraction of what people assume

🇹🇷 Turkey

From Istanbul's chaos to Cappadocia's silence — same country, different worlds

Solo Culture Hiking Rent a car

Turkey is ten countries in one. I started in Istanbul — overwhelming, electric, ancient. Then I drove east. The landscape changes slowly at first, then all at once. By the time I reached Cappadocia I felt like I'd travelled centuries, not kilometres. The hot air balloons at sunrise over the valleys is one of the few things that's exactly as good as the photos suggest.

  • Watched sunrise over Cappadocia from a rooftop — no balloon needed
  • Hiked the Ihlara Valley alone — saw nobody for hours
  • Found a hammam in Istanbul that's been running since 1584
  • Drove the Black Sea coast — completely underrated
